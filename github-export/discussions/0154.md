### Comment 1 — @earth2marsh · 2024-09-24 17:08Z

Today we discussed @handrews 's recent [blog post and diagram iteration](https://modern-json-schema.com/analyzing-the-openapi-tooling-ecosystem). The discussion primarily focused on understanding the different colors, which we tentatively called the various "concerns" (as in the separation of concerns). 

Gray, yellow, and pink (at least pink below the parsing line in the diagram) fall into the space where we might consider within our overall mandate. It was proposed that it would be generally useful to provide a tool that could validate that the semantics of a description were preserved as a document was converted between different spec versions (i.e., upcast and then downcast). This means not "losing" information in such a transformation, implying that deprecated features would need to be represented (likely via extensions). [NB: while writing up notes, it struck me that validation of a well-formed document should likewise be within our mandate!]

Video is [available](https://zoom.us/rec/share/d7-PhUvI-JYtJaA05Iw3pcKxsVwESYO0RurEtWh3G2qpJY3HfnqBJpn9uH3Gqtcs.hdAM4vSy-mQdhVju).
